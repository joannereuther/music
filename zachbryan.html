Music Type

Unlike Taylor Swift and Noah Kahan, Zach Bryan's music is easy to discribe, country. His songs fit in to the country music style perfectly, with his simple harmonies, stringed insturments, repeated choruses, and narrative lyrics.

History of Zach Bryan

Originally from Oklahoma City, Zach Bryan grew up with country music and traditions. Being surrounded with country music, he started to post on social media singing and playing covers and his own songs. After his high school graduation, Zach moved to Nashville to presue his music career. Over the years Zach would post him singing online using his iPhone, this gained him some fans, but it was his single "Heading South" that went viral and gained him a larger following. In 2019, he released his first album, DeAnn, named after his late mother; the album reached double platinum sales levels. Not to long after, he released his second album, "Elisabeth" in 2020. In 2022 he released both Amerian Heartbreak and All My Homies Hate Ticketmaster, which had his live set list from his show at Red Rocks. It was his songs "Something in the orange," "Heading South," and "Dawns" that gained him his major popularity. In 2023 he relased his self titled album, which he is currently touring internationally.

Why I like Zach Bryan

Before Listening to Zach Bryan's Music, I did not typically listen to country music. It was his music that introduced me to the genrea and made me enjoy listening to country music in general. I also appreciate his lyrics and the meanings behind them because they tell a story about his or people he knows experiances.
